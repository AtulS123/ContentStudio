# Story Script: Kalakritiyon ka Bharat

## Scene 1: The Classroom
**Visual:** A large school hall. Students sitting excitedly. A teacher standing at the front.
**Narration:** The students gathered in the big hall for the "Ek Bharat, Shreshtha Bharat" programme.
**Dialogue:**
*   **Teacher:** Are you ready to meet your friends from all over the country?
*   **Students:** Yes!
*   **Renu:** It is to know that we all belong to one nation. Our unity in diversity makes us wonderful.

## Scene 2: Uttarakhand - Aipan Art
**Visual:** A video screen lights up. Aakansha (girl) appears. Background of Uttarakhand mountains. She shows white designs on a red floor.
**Narration:** The video started. First was Aakansha from Uttarakhand.
**Voiceover (Aakansha):** "Pranam! I am Aakansha. This is Aipan, our folk art. We draw it with white rice paste on red clay walls for festivals."

## Scene 3: Odisha - Dhokra Craft
**Visual:** Priyaranjan (boy) appears. He holds a metal figure. Montage of making a clay model, covering with wax, and pouring molten metal.
**Narration:** Next was Priyaranjan from Odisha.
**Voiceover (Priyaranjan):** "Suprabhat! I am Priyaranjan. My tribe practices the 4000-year-old Dhokra metal craft. We use the lost-wax technique to create beautiful brass figures."

## Scene 4: Kerala - Coconut Shell Craft
**Visual:** Chitra (girl) appears. She is polishing a coconut shell bowl.
**Narration:** Then came Chitra from Kerala.
**Voiceover (Chitra):** "Suprabhaatam! I am Chitra. We make eco-friendly crafts from coconut shells. We clean, smooth, and polish them to make bowls and jewellery."

## Scene 5: Andhra Pradesh - Kondapalli Toys
**Visual:** Balamurali (boy) appears. He is painting a wooden toy of a bullock cart.
**Narration:** Finally, Balamurali from Andhra Pradesh.
**Voiceover (Balamurali):** "Subhodayam! I am Balamurali. In Kondapalli, we make toys from soft wood. We carve, join with tamarind paste, and paint them with vegetable dyes."

## Scene 6: Unity in Diversity
**Visual:** Split screen showing all four children with their art forms. The class applauds.
**Narration:** The students clapped. They learned how different states have unique and beautiful traditions, yet they are all part of one India.
