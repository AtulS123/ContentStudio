# Story Script: Change of Heart

## Scene 1: The Sore Loser
**Visual:** School playground. Prabhat playing marbles or a game. He loses and looks furious/terrible.
**Narration:** Prabhat always wanted to win. He couldn't bear losing, not even at marbles. If he thought he might lose, he wouldn't even play.

## Scene 2: The New Rival
**Visual:** A new student, Surya, enters. He plays badminton and is excellent. Prabhat watches him intently.
**Narration:** A new student, Surya, joined the school. He was an excellent badminton player. Prabhat waited for the monthly friendly match to challenge him.

## Scene 3: The Match
**Visual:** The badminton match. Surya is relaxed, smiling, cracking jokes. Prabhat is tense and serious.
**Narration:** On the day of the match, Prabhat was serious, but Surya was relaxed and joking. Surya played like a phenomenon, winning points easily while laughing.

## Scene 4: The Cheat
**Visual:** Surya looks away to laugh at a joke. Prabhat quickly changes the scoreboard to favor himself.
**Narration:** Surya was paying so little attention to the score that Prabhat managed to cheat. He changed the scoreboard while Surya was looking away.

## Scene 5: The Hollow Victory
**Visual:** Prabhat wins. He celebrates loudly. Surya smiles, shakes his hand, and says "It's been fun."
**Narration:** Prabhat won by cheating. He made a big deal of it, but Surya just smiled and said, "It's been fun. We should play again."

## Scene 6: The Realization
**Visual:** Night time. Prabhat tossing in bed, unable to sleep. Flashback to Surya playing basketball badly but still smiling.
**Narration:** That night, Prabhat couldn't sleep. He realized Surya didn't care about winning or losing. The next day, he saw Surya playing basketball badly but still enjoying every moment.

## Scene 7: The Change
**Visual:** Montage. Prabhat playing hide and seek, losing but smiling. Prabhat joking during badminton. Other kids whispering "Good fun playing with Prabhat."
**Narration:** Prabhat realized that enjoying the game was what mattered. He started playing for fun, joking, and even accepting defeat with a smile. Everyone noticed his great sporting spirit.
