# Story Script: Spices that Heal Us

## Scene 1: The Letter
**Visual:** Vikram and Vaibhavi reading a letter from their grandmother (Daadi).
**Narration:** Vikram and Vaibhavi received a letter from their Daadi. She was happy they had recovered from their illness using her natural cures.

## Scene 2: Turmeric (Haldi)
**Visual:** Daadi in her kitchen (flashback/imagination). She holds a spoonful of yellow turmeric powder.
**Narration:** Daadi wrote about the magic spices in her kitchen. First, Haldi or Turmeric.
**Voiceover (Daadi):** "Haldi improves energy and digestion. It also reduces body pain."

## Scene 3: Fenugreek (Methi)
**Visual:** Daadi soaking brown fenugreek seeds in water overnight. Drinking the water in the morning.
**Narration:** Next was Methi or Fenugreek.
**Voiceover (Daadi):** "Methi helps control sugar levels and weight. I soak the seeds overnight and drink the water in the morning."

## Scene 4: Cumin (Jeera) and Asafoetida (Heeng)
**Visual:** A baby crying with a tummy ache. Daadi applying Heeng water on the tummy. The baby smiles.
**Narration:** She remembered when they were babies.
**Voiceover (Daadi):** "When you had tummy gas, I used Heeng water for relief. And Jeera water helps with sleep and digestion."

## Scene 5: Cinnamon, Clove, and Ginger
**Visual:** Close up of Cinnamon sticks, Cloves, and fresh Ginger.
**Narration:** For pains and colds.
**Voiceover (Daadi):** "Dalchini (Cinnamon) and Laung (Clove) help with toothaches. And Adrak (Ginger)? It's been used for 4000 years for coughs, colds, and pain!"

## Scene 6: The After-Meal Digestives
**Visual:** A family finishing a meal and eating Fennel (Saunf) and Cardamom (Elaichi).
**Narration:** Finally, the tasty ones.
**Voiceover (Daadi):** "That's why we eat Saunf and Elaichi after meals. They help digestion and cure bad breath."

## Scene 7: The Advice
**Visual:** Vikram and Vaibhavi in the kitchen, looking at the spice jars with new respect.
**Narration:** Daadi finished her letter with important advice.
**Voiceover (Daadi):** "Natural cures are passed down for generations. But remember, always consult an elder before using them!"
