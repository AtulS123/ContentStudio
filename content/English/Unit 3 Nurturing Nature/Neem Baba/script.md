# Story Script: Neem Baba

## Scene 1: The Meeting
**Visual:** A courtyard with a large, old Neem tree. Amber (a young girl) returns from school and sits in its shade.
**Narration:** Amber returned from school and sat under the old Neem tree in her courtyard. She felt the tree whispering to her.
**Dialogue:**
*   **Amber:** Namaste! I am Amber. May I talk to you?
*   **Neem Baba:** Sure, dear Amber. I know you. You played in my shade as a child. Call me Neem Baba.

## Scene 2: The History
**Visual:** Flashback or map showing India, Myanmar, Africa, America.
**Narration:** Amber asked about his origins.
**Dialogue:**
*   **Neem Baba:** I am very old. Born millions of years ago in India or Myanmar. Now my family is all over the world—Asia, Africa, even America.
*   **Amber:** That's wonderful! Who named you?
*   **Neem Baba:** Iranians gave me the name 'Neem'. In Sanskrit, I am Arishta—the one who cures diseases.

## Scene 3: The Healer
**Visual:** Amber remembering her brother with measles lying on neem leaves.
**Narration:** Amber remembered how Neem helped her family.
**Dialogue:**
*   **Amber:** When my brother had measles, the doctor said to put neem leaves on his bed. Why?
*   **Neem Baba:** My leaves destroy germs and soothe itching. My bark, flowers, and fruits also make medicines.

## Scene 4: The Farmer's Friend
**Visual:** A farmer spraying neem water on crops. Healthy crops, no bugs.
**Narration:** Amber asked if he helped farmers too.
**Dialogue:**
*   **Neem Baba:** Yes! Neem seed powder mixed with water stops locusts and termites. It saves crops and increases yield.
*   **Amber:** And it stops mosquitoes too?
*   **Neem Baba:** Yes, it stops mosquitoes from breeding in stagnant water.

## Scene 5: Daily Uses
**Visual:** Montage of neem toothpaste, soap, furniture.
**Narration:** Neem Baba explained his other uses.
**Dialogue:**
*   **Neem Baba:** My oil is used in soap and toothpaste. My wood is termite-proof for furniture. I purify the air.
*   **Amber:** You do so much! I will discover even more uses when I grow up. Thank you, Neem Baba!
*   **Neem Baba:** Bless you, my child.
