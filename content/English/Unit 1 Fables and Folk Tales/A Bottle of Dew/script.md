# Story Script: A Bottle of Dew

## Scene 1: The Lazy Landlord
**Visual:** A grand house with large, neglected fields. Rama Natha (30s, rich clothes) is lounging, reading an old book about magic. His wife, Madhumati, looks worried.
**Narration:** Rama Natha was the son of a rich landlord. When his father died, he left him large tracts of land. But Rama Natha didn't spend a single day looking after his land. He had a funny ideaâ€”he believed there was a magic potion that could turn any object into gold.

## Scene 2: The Sage's Arrival
**Visual:** A famous sage, Mahipati, arrives in town. Rama Natha rushes to meet him, bowing respectfully.
**Narration:** One day, a famous sage named Mahipati came to town. Rama Natha became his follower and asked, "Is there really a potion that turns things to gold?" The sage smiled, "Yes, I heard of it in the Himalayas. But it is difficult to make."

## Scene 3: The Secret Ingredient
**Visual:** The sage whispering to Rama Natha. Rama Natha looks excited but then worried.
**Narration:** The sage said, "You must plant a banana plant and water it yourself. In winter, the morning dew forms on the leaves. You must collect this dew in a bottle. When you have five liters, bring it to me. I will chant magic words to turn it into the potion."

## Scene 4: The Calculation
**Visual:** Rama Natha calculating on his fingers. He looks distressed.
**Narration:** Rama Natha worried, "Winter is only for a few months. It will take years to collect five liters!" The sage replied, "You can plant as many trees as you want. But remember, you must collect the dew with your own hands."

## Scene 5: The Transformation
**Visual:** Montage of 6 years.
1.  Rama Natha clearing his empty fields.
2.  Planting rows of banana trees.
3.  Winter morning: Rama Natha carefully collecting dew drops into a bottle.
4.  Madhumati taking baskets of bananas to the market and selling them.
5.  The plantation growing huge and green.
**Narration:** Rama Natha went home and cleaned his fields. He planted rows of banana plants and tended them carefully. For six years, he collected dew every winter morning. Meanwhile, Madhumati sold the rich harvest in the market.

## Scene 6: The "Magic" Moment
**Visual:** Rama Natha, looking older but stronger, presenting a full bottle of dew to the sage. The sage mutters over it.
**Narration:** Finally, he had five liters. He took the bottle to the sage. The sage smiled, muttered something, and said, "Try it out."

## Scene 7: The Disappointment
**Visual:** Rama Natha sprinkling the water on a copper vessel. Nothing happens. He looks angry.
**Narration:** Rama Natha sprinkled the drops on a copper vessel. Nothing happened! "You cheated me!" he shouted. "I wasted six years!"

## Scene 8: The Real Gold
**Visual:** The sage smiles and calls Madhumati. She opens a large box filled with gold coins. Rama Natha is stunned.
**Narration:** The sage smiled and called Madhumati. She opened a box full of gold coins. "There is no magic potion," the sage said. "While you worked hard, your wife sold the bananas. This gold is the fruit of your hard work."

## Scene 9: The Lesson Learned
**Visual:** Rama Natha smiling, looking at his hands and then at the plantation. He hugs his wife and bows to the sage.
**Narration:** Rama Natha understood the wisdom. He realized that hard work was the real magic, and from that day on, he worked even harder on his plantation.
