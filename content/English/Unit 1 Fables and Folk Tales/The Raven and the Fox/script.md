# Story Script: The Raven and the Fox

## Scene 1: The Prize
**Visual:** A forest setting. Mr. Raven is perched high on a tree limb. He holds a large, delicious morsel (cheese or meat) in his beak. He looks very pleased with himself.
**Narration:** Mr. Raven was perched upon a limb, holding a delicious morsel in his great big beak. It was a treat that anyone would go far to seek.

## Scene 2: The Observer
**Visual:** Reynard the Fox trots by below. He stops, looks up, and spots the food. His eyes widen, and he licks his lips.
**Narration:** Reynard the Fox looked up at him. He saw the food and his mouth watered. He decided he must have it.

## Scene 3: The Flattery
**Visual:** The Fox puts on a charming smile and speaks up to the Raven. The Raven looks down, listening.
**Narration:** The Fox said in admiring tones, "My word! Sir Raven, you are a handsome bird! Such feathers! They shine so beautifully."

## Scene 4: The Temptation
**Visual:** The Raven puffs up his chest, looking proud. The Fox continues, gesturing with his paws.
**Narration:** "If you would only sing," continued the Fox, "The birds of these woods would call you King! I am sure your voice is as sweet as your looks."

## Scene 5: The Fall
**Visual:** The Raven, swelling with pride, opens his beak wide to let out a loud "Caw!". The morsel falls from his beak.
**Narration:** The Raven forgot that his voice was just a croak. Filled with foolish pride, he opened his beak to sing. And down fell the morsel the Fox had eyed!

## Scene 6: The Lesson
**Visual:** The Fox snaps up the food instantly. He chews it happily while looking up at the stunned Raven.
**Narration:** "Ha-ha!" laughed the Fox. "And now you know. Ignore sweet words that make you glow. Pride, my friend, is rather unwise."

## Scene 7: The Aftermath
**Visual:** The Fox trots away, full and happy. The Raven sits alone on the branch, looking sadder but wiser.
**Narration:** The Fox ran off with his prize, leaving the Raven with a surprise lesson: Do not let vanity cost you your treasure.
